AIM Research Support Facility

The AIM Research Support Facility (RSF), based at The Alan Turing Institute with collaborators from Swansea University and the University of Edinburgh, offers AI and advanced data science support to the research teams part of the NIHR's Artificial Intelligence for Multiple Long-Term Conditions (AIM) project.

Visit our Events page to find information and register for upcoming events.

Accessible, Research Ready Data.

Data wranglers, experts in data curation, quality control, and reproducibility, work with the Research Collaborations to align datasets and standards.

Community Building and Training.

Establish connections between AIM researchers, and deliver training and mentorship to researchers that support the delivery of open source outputs.

Research Sustainability and Legacy.

Work with researcher teams to embed outputs in existing clinical and academic, and engage with policy makers to coordinate a long term investment in MLTC research

Reproducible, Secure and Interoperable Infrastructure.

Bring together research collaborations across the UK together within a trusted research environment to facilitate data, software and analysis sharing.

Patient and Public Involvement and Engagement.

Foster an embedded PPIE community focused on supporting and empowering people with lived experience of MLTC to co-create research alongside academic partners.
